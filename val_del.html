Validators & Delegators

(Source of income)

Blockchain is a database that contains information in the form of interconnected blocks. Blockchain is a distributed database because it is stored on different computers connected to the network. A computer with software code installed on it is called a node. A node that stores a copy of the blockchain ensures the security and reliability of the network.

A blockchain network validator is a node or server that ensures the functioning and security of a distributed computing network. They verify and confirm the legitimacy of transactions, and also participate in the mining or staking process (depending on the blockchain network used).

Validators are responsible for adding new blocks to the blockchain, thereby ensuring that all data on the network is recorded and stored. Their role is to ensure the integrity and consistency of the distributed ledger, making the blockchain secure, reliable and decentralized.

Proof of Stake (POS) means that you confirm the correct block in decentralized network using your crypto coins. Staking involves validators or delegators who have locked tokens on their contracts, so they can be randomly selected by the protocol at specific intervals to create a block.

After the new block is signed, validators and participants receive a reward, which is evenly distributed in a staking pool. This means that at the end of each validating cycle (or epoch), your stake increases, generating you passive income.
